# Campaign Types — Email Drip Templates

## Welcome Sequence (5 emails — new subscriber)

### Email 1: Welcome + Brand Story (send immediately)
- Subject: "Welcome to [Brand] — here's what to expect"
- Preview: "A quick note from [founder name / team]"
- Content: Founder story, brand mission, what subscriber will get, one proof point
- CTA: "Explore our products" / "Learn about the service"

### Email 2: Education (Day 2–3)
- Subject: "[Problem] — what most people get wrong"
- Preview: "The thing nobody tells you about [pain point]"
- Content: Core educational piece, mechanism explanation, no hard sell
- CTA: "Read the full guide" (blog or product page)

### Email 3: Social Proof (Day 5–6)
- Subject: "[Name] used to [problem]. Then they tried this."
- Preview: "60,000 athletes. Here's what they found."
- Content: Customer testimonial or case study, specific results
- CTA: "See the product" / "Compare packages"

### Email 4: Product Feature (Day 8–9)
- Subject: "The science behind [product / service]"
- Preview: "Here's exactly how it works"
- Content: Mechanism deep-dive, credentials, proof points
- CTA: "Shop now" / "Book your morning"

### Email 5: Offer (Day 12–14)
- Subject: "A welcome offer — just for new subscribers"
- Preview: "This is only available for the next [X] days"
- Content: Specific offer, risk reversal (30-day return / easy booking), hard CTA
- CTA: "Claim your offer" / "Start with a question"

## Product Launch Sequence (5–7 emails)

### Email 1: Teaser (1 week before launch)
- Subject: "Something new is coming [Date]"
- Content: Build anticipation, hint at product/service benefit, no reveal

### Email 2: Problem Framing (5 days before)
- Subject: "[Pain point] — you're not alone"
- Content: Deep problem articulation, why existing solutions fail

### Email 3: Launch Day
- Subject: "It's here — [Product Name]"
- Content: Full product reveal, all proof points, launch offer

### Email 4: Social Proof (Day 2–3)
- Subject: "What customers are saying already"
- Content: Early testimonials, UGC, reactions

### Email 5: Objection Kill (Day 5)
- Subject: "Still on the fence? Here's what to know"
- Content: Address top 3 objections, risk reversal

### Email 6: Last Chance (Day 7 if limited offer)
- Subject: "Last chance — [offer] ends tonight"
- Content: Urgency (only if real), final CTA

---

## Re-engagement Sequence (3 emails)

### Email 1: Check-in
- Subject: "Still interested in [Brand]?"
- Content: "We noticed you haven't been around. Here's what's new."

### Email 2: Best Content
- Subject: "Our most popular resource — for you"
- Content: High-value content piece (most-read blog, best video)

### Email 3: Last Call
- Subject: "We'll remove you from our list unless..."
- Content: One clear offer + "Click here to stay on our list"
- Note: Remove non-openers after this — improves deliverability

## RTP Tennis email cadence note
Send frequency: Max 2x/week during campaigns, 1x/week general
Best send times: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am or 7–9pm (Canada time zones)

## Kmedtest email cadence note
Send frequency: Max 1–2x/week (considered purchase, don't over-message)
Best send times: Weekdays 8–10am, weekend 10am–12pm
Tone: always acknowledge "this is a big decision" framing
