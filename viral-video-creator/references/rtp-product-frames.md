### What it does
Attaches to the tennis racquet frame. Absorbs vibration from ball impact before it travels up the frame into the wrist, forearm, and elbow. Uses Sorbothane — the same material used in NASA equipment and military applications — to interrupt the injury cycle.

### The mechanism (for MOFU content)
Tennis elbow isn't a muscle problem — it's a cumulative vibration problem. Every ball strike sends a shockwave through the frame. Conventional string dampeners only reduce string vibration (sound). They do nothing for frame vibration. The ShockSorb Dampener attaches at the frame and intercepts the actual impact vibration before it reaches the arm.

### What it does
Tennis-specific socks with dual-sided polyurethane grip coating on the sole AND the inner foot contact area. Eliminates foot sliding inside the shoe during lateral movements. Official socks of the APP Pickleball Tour.

## PRODUCT 4 — WASP Grip Strips

### What it does
Adhesive grip strips for the racquet handle. Eliminates racquet slippage in the hand during shots — particularly in humid conditions, during long matches when sweat is a factor, and for players with hand strength issues.

### The mechanism (for MOFU content)
When the racquet slips in the hand, the body compensates by gripping tighter. This white-knuckling increases forearm tension on every shot — accelerating tennis elbow risk, reducing feel, and creating inconsistent stroke mechanics. WASP Grip Strips eliminate the slip reflex — allowing a relaxed, biomechanically correct grip.
